Qissa 2022 Speakers

Arushee Rajeev

From Dusk to Dawn
"Everyone makes mistakes, but that doesn't mean they have to pay for it with the rest of their lives. Sometimes good people make bad choices but it doesn’t mean that they are bad people, it just means they are human." This is a story of an individual who wants to move forward, accept reality, and turn her weaknesses into strengths without being judged - a story of realisation and acceptance of one’s own identity.
Trigger Warnings - Depression and Anxiety

Anna

Kashmakash
We think about it, we crave it, but seldom do we admit to it. Sometimes, all we need is to feel safe and protected in our lives. Kashmakash is a state of mind understood through the story of a childhood pervaded by love, loss, and bubbles of innocence burst too soon. What happens when you are forced to grow up too fast?
Trigger Warnings - Death, anxiety, sexual abuse, misogyny.

Ashwani Garg

Zindagi in a box
What do years of familial derision, a complete change in environment, and the sudden loss of a friend do to a person? And what compels them to soldier on when it feels like there’s nothing left to live for? This is the story of a person recognizing the box surrounding them, and their process of breaking out of it. This is the story of a person on the brink of despair, and the unexpectedly supportive people in their life who brought them back from it.
Trigger Warnings - Suicide attempt(s)

Tea Cup

"Was it my Fault?"
How does a six year old deal with abuse she did not even know she was enduring? How does she fend for herself in a family that should have protected her? "You'll have a better story to tell. You can be shaped, dented but not broken." In a compilation of personal experiences, titled, "Was it my Fault?" Teacup talks about her journey towards healing - a journey consisting of ups and downs, highs and lows, and a life graph that is never constant.
Trigger Warnings - Sexual Abuse, Verbal Abuse, Bodyshaming, Bullying, Family Feuds.

Ice

Monster under the bed
How does an only child cope with learning their grandparents have a disease no one dares talk about? How does a college student care for two chronically ill people through the pandemic, and what does it cost them? And how do they grapple with abuse at the hands of the people they’re supposed to love? Monster under the bed is a story about HIV/AIDs, stigma, the faulty healthcare system and the desperation of a child under years of duress.
Trigger Warnings - Illness, death, domestic violence

Aniruddh Shankar

Invisible Privilege
How does life change once you shift to college? Stepping out of your comfort zone and learning to take responsibility for ourselves is something that we've all been through. Somewhere along the way, we realize the privilege we've had, and try to break free from it. This coming of age story is about a guy trying to adjust to the world, without all the privileges and luxuries backing him up, and how music has played a big role in his life, helping him evolve into the person he is today.

Karanveer Kumar

Trailing: A memoir
We knew we were different, even from our elementary school days. We were the class clowns; we engaged with people differently. We knew there was something out there that was meant for us.

SAJ

With Love, With All My Heart
This is a story of an average kid making his way through an extraordinary world; that of a boy, not turning into a man, but instead into a content, impulsive, outrageously annoying and nauseatingly optimistic teenager. The story is not of happy endings, but rather of justified sad ones. A story, not of emotions within the moment, instead of learnings that come after. This is a story of what made him who he is, a tale of where he's from, a testament to where he's going. This is his story... and it's one worth being told.
Trigger Warnings - Sexual Assault, Self harm, Bullying, Body Shaming, Suicidal Ideation, Family Feuds
